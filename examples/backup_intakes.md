# Backup Project Intakes

Showed up without a project? Pick the one closest to your world and run
with it. Or use it as a model and swap in your own details.

These are written the way a good intake should be: the "what better looks
like" line is an *outcome* ("they can X without Y"), not a feature ("build
a dashboard"). When you fill in your own, aim for that.

---

## Option A — Prospect demo script (Specialist)

*Pairs with `context_A_demo_script.md` — the use-case catalog and sample
prospect you'll assemble from.*

**1. The problem, in one sentence.**
Every prospect needs a demo tailored to their identity challenges, and I
rebuild the narrative from scratch each time even though I'm recombining the
same core use-cases.

**2. Who has this problem? (the persona)**
An Okta pre-sales Specialist who runs tailored product demos — choosing which
identity use-cases to show and weaving them into a story that lands for a given
prospect's priorities.

**3. The pain. (2-3 bullets)**
- I reassemble the same building-block use-cases (SSO, MFA, lifecycle,
  governance) into a fresh narrative for every prospect, by hand.
- Tailoring to a prospect's industry and priorities eats time, and I start
  from a blank page instead of a known-good structure.
- Demo scripts drift in quality and consistency across the team — everyone
  builds their own.

**4. What does "better" look like?**
A Specialist can turn a prospect profile into a coherent, tailored demo script
— the right use-cases, in the right order, with the right talking points — in
an hour instead of half a day, without starting from scratch.

## Option B — Recurring questionnaire / RFP response (Advisor)

**1. The problem, in one sentence.**
I answer the same security and technical questions across every RFP and
vendor questionnaire, by hand, from a blank page each time.

**2. Who has this problem? (the persona)**
A pre-sales Advisor who owns the technical and security sections of RFP and
questionnaire responses for inbound deals.

**3. The pain. (2-3 bullets)**
- Roughly the same questions recur across deals, but I re-answer them
  manually every time.
- Answers drift and contradict each other across responses - there's no
  single source of truth.
- Turnaround is slow enough that it delays deals and frustrates the AEs
  waiting on me.

**4. What does "better" look like?**
An Advisor can produce a consistent, accurate first draft of a recurring
questionnaire in an hour, with each answer traceable to an approved source,
instead of starting from scratch.

## Option C — Discovery notes to solution sketch (Architect)

**1. The problem, in one sentence.**
After a technical discovery call I have a pile of messy notes and have to
turn them into a coherent solution proposal the prospect will actually
read.

**2. Who has this problem? (the persona)**
A pre-sales Architect who runs discovery calls and produces the follow-up
architecture sketch that shapes how the deal moves forward.

**3. The pain. (2-3 bullets)**
- Synthesizing raw notes into a structured proposal is slow and usually
  happens after hours.
- Requirements mentioned on the call get lost on the way to the written
  proposal.
- Every Architect formats proposals differently, so quality is uneven
  across the team.

**4. What does "better" look like?**
An Architect can turn discovery notes into a structured, consistent
solution sketch the same day as the call, without requirements falling
through the cracks.
