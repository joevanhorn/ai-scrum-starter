# VISION — AI-Assisted Pre-Sales Operating System

*A finished VISION, for reference. This is the one used to build the workshop
itself — the method, dogfooding the method.*

## Product
A 45-minute interactive session teaching pre-sales colleagues a scrum-inspired,
three-role system for working with AI assistants.

## Personas
- **Primary:** Pre-sales Specialists, Architects, Advisors who already use AI
  assistants reactively (one chat, one task) and want a repeatable system.
- **Secondary:** Colleagues who've heard the results and want to copy the method.
- **NOT for:** people brand-new to AI assistants — they need a different session
  first.

## Pain
- Treating AI as smart autocomplete instead of a team to run.
- Going serial when work could be parallel.
- Re-inventing the approach every project; no reusable artifacts.
- Can't articulate to a teammate what they're actually doing differently.
- Stalling when context gets long or tasks get too big to finish in one chat.

## Outcomes
- "I know what artifacts to make before I open a chat."
- "I have a template I'll actually reuse next week."
- "I felt the three-role loop work on something I care about."
- "I can explain the system to my own team in one paragraph."

## Non-Goals
- Teaching scrum theory.
- Finishing a feature in the room.
- A prompting-technique deep dive.
- Tool or model recommendations.

## Backlog (prioritized)
1. Open frame: autocomplete vs. system (4 min).
2. Exercise 1 — define before delegate: plan a sprint with the Architect (15 min).
3. Exercise 2 — kick off the Scrum Master and run a sprint (20 min).
4. Close: failure modes + take-home (the remaining minutes).

## Definition of Done
- Every participant has a filled-in VISION for their own project.
- Every participant has kicked off a Scrum Master and seen it dispatch (or
  correctly bounce) a sprint.
- At least one volunteer commits to trying it on a real initiative.

## Constraints
- 45 minutes, hard stop.
- Laptops + working Claude Code per person; many on a LiteLLM gateway.
- Mixed experience with AI assistants.
- Solo facilitator.
